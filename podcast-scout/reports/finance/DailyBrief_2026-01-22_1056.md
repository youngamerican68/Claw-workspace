#### 4.2 "Winner's Curse" in Private Markets
- **Theme**: The "winner's curse" phenomenon, where the winner of an auction overpays, is prevalent in competitive bidding environments. This effect is amplified with more bidders.
- **The Creative Play**:
    - **Short Private Equity-Backed Companies (Post-Acquisition):** Identify private equity firms that frequently win highly competitive auctions for companies, particularly in sectors prone to overvaluation. Short the public equity of companies these PE firms subsequently take public, or consider shorting the PE firms themselves if their strategy relies heavily on such acquisitions.
    - **Long "Under-the-Radar" Assets/Businesses:** Focus on identifying assets or businesses that are likely to be overlooked by large pools of capital or are in less competitive acquisition environments, thereby avoiding the winner's curse.
- **Why It's a Long Shot**:
    - **Shorting PE-backed companies:** Requires deep understanding of private market valuations and the specific dynamics of PE exits, which are often illiquid. Identifying which specific acquisitions were subject to the winner's curse is difficult post-factum.
    - **Long "Under-the-Radar" Assets:** Discovery is challenging, and liquidity can be low. The "under-the-radar" nature means less analyst coverage and potentially higher transaction costs.
- **The Payoff If Right**:
    - **Shorting PE-backed companies:** Significant alpha if the market eventually recognizes the overpayment made during the acquisition phase, leading to underperformance post-IPO.
    - **Long "Under-the-Radar" Assets:** Potential for outsized returns if these assets are eventually "discovered" by the broader market or larger strategic buyers.
- **Supporting Quote(s)**:
    - Alex Imas: "The winner's curse is essentially the fact that, look, nobody really knows how much money is in the jar... The key result from the winner's curse is that the winner is not random. It's the person who overestimated what's in the jar."
    - Alex Imas: "It's more prevalent, the more people that are bidding.
