**Our Edge**: The scale of committed utility capacity for data centers (110 GW firm commitments, PUE adjusted) far exceeds even bullish third-party demand forecasts (50 GW needed by 2030, 110 GW needed by 2035). The market is not adequately differentiating between regions or utility-specific ratepayer protection mechanisms.
**Signal Components**:
*   **Capacity vs. Demand Mismatch:** Committed supply significantly outstripping forecast demand.
*   **Regulatory/Political Risk:** Unprotected rate payers bearing costs of overbuild.
*   **Credit Risk:** Utilities taking on debt for potentially underutilized assets.
**Catalysts**:
*   Utility earnings calls revealing slowed data center demand or increased capital expenditure write-downs (ongoing).
*   Regulatory bodies denying rate increases or mandating shareholder absorption of costs.
*   Increased public scrutiny and political pressure on utility rate hikes in affected regions.
*   Data center tenants defaulting or cancelling commitments as AI efficiency gains reduce power needs.
**Risks & Falsification**:
*   Data center demand accelerates dramatically beyond current bullish forecasts (e.g., beyond 110 GW by 2030).
*   Utilities successfully pass all capital expenditure costs to ratepayers without significant political or regulatory pushback.
*   New, unforeseen secular demand drivers emerge for electricity.
*   Utilities are able to significantly reduce build-out costs or adapt infrastructure for other uses rapidly.
**Implementation**: Consider shorting XLU or specific utility companies heavily exposed to data center buildouts in regions without strong ratepayer protection (e.g., Southeast). Avoid Texas and Midwest ISO utilities as they have unique market structures or are already prepared.
