**3. Conventional Wisdom**: Wikipedia is the holy grail for online credibility.
- **The Contrarian View**: Niche-specific, highly relevant mentions on reputable industry sites are more powerful than a generalist Wikipedia page for AI credibility. "The obsession with Wikipedia is a mistake." (Jason Barnard)
- **The Logic**: AI prioritizes explicit, specific, and consistent information within a niche. "If you can get industry and geolocation specific, you'll win in the game." (Jason Barnard)
- **How to Bet on This**: Identify 3-5 highly relevant industry-specific websites or associations. Focus on getting mentioned or featured there, ensuring consistency with your "entity home website."

### 7. FAILURE PATTERNS

Pitfalls to avoid on the indie hacker journey.

**1. The Trap**: Over-optimizing your AI system before shipping.
- **Why It's Tempting**: The desire for a perfect, ultra-complex agent system with sub-agents, hooks, and skills. "Looking at Twitter, you see a lot of people optimizing or potentially over-optimizing their own system." (Ben Tossell)
- **The Reality**: You get stuck in analysis paralysis and never ship. "Just get started." (Ben Tossell)
- **The Alternative**: Start simple, build ahead of your capability, and learn from errors. "Build, fail forward, and keep shipping." (Ben Tossell)

**2. The Trap**: Starting with a broad market and trying to be "all things to all people."
- **Why It's Tempting**: The allure of a massive total addressable market (TAM). "The mistake that 95%, 99% of businesses and SEOs and digital marketers make is I'm gonna start at the top of the funnel with awareness, with these big, big volume keywords." (Jason Barnard)
- **The Reality**: You get diluted, fail to build deep credibility, and get outcompeted by niche players.
- **The Alternative**: "Start with a hyper niche and build your way out." (Jason Barnard). Dominate a small segment, then expand.

**3. The Trap**: Ignoring the "why now" or assuming your unique advantage is permanent.
- **Why It's Tempting**: Believing your initial lead in a fast-moving market is defensible. "If your peer strategy is like, it's a gold rush, I'm going to get their fastest, then you have to be charging incredibly hard." (Dylan Field)
- **The Reality**: Competitors catch up quickly, especially with AI. "Once somebody proves that it's capable, it seems to not be that hard for other people to be able to catch up, even people with far less resources." (Marc Andreessen)
- **The Alternative**: Continuously innovate, focus on defensible craft/brand/storytelling, and be prepared for rapid market shifts. "Good enough isn't good enough anymore." (Dylan Field)
