### 7. CONTRARIAN TAKES

*   **Mainstream View**: Venture capital success is primarily about "picking" the smartest deals and having visionary investors.
    *   **Contrarian View**: "Winning" the deal (having the brand, platform, and resources to attract the best founders) is a much bigger factor than picking. A great winner with average picking skills will outperform a great picker who can't win deals.
    *   **How to Bet on It**: For indie hackers, this means prioritizing building a strong personal brand and a "platform" (even a micro-platform like a niche blog, community, or unique tool) that attracts opportunities, rather than solely focusing on becoming a "genius" at identifying opportunities. Your ability to *attract* customers/partners is more valuable than your ability to *find* them.
    *   **Supporting Quote**: "winning it is a much bigger percentage of that equation than people like in VC world like to give credit for it... if you can't win it, then you're still never gonna have good returns." - Ben Horowitz

*   **Mainstream View**: To make a career change or pursue a big goal, you need to first accumulate skills, capital, and network through linear steps (the "deferred life plan").
    *   **Contrarian View**: This "deferred life plan" is often a procrastination tactic. It's better to directly pursue the thing you truly want to do, and the necessary skills/capital/network will follow as you engage with the actual work.
    *   **How to Bet on It**: Stop over-planning and over-preparing. Identify the smallest possible step to *do* the thing you want, and take it. The "prerequisites" will reveal themselves and be acquired more effectively through direct action.
    *   **Supporting Quote**: "I don't believe in the deferred life plan. Like I don't believe you need to do all these prerequisites to go do the thing you want to do. I think more often you're better served just going and doing the thing you really want to do if you really want to go do it." - Dan Clancy (CEO of Twitch, as quoted by Sean)
