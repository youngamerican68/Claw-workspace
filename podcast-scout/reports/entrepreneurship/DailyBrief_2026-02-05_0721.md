### 6. CONTRARIAN TAKES

*   **Mainstream View**: AI will commoditize specialized skills and knowledge, making everyone an instant expert.
*   **Contrarian View**: "The models are going to be so good that you just type anything you want in and get exactly what you want. And it would just commoditize any specialized skill or knowledge. And it's just not true." (Kieran, Transcript 9) Instead, AI makes *real specialists more* valuable by allowing them to build systems that scale their expertise.
*   **How to Bet on It**: Build tools that empower specialists (marketers, sales reps, consultants) to codify their unique knowledge into AI workflows and systems, rather than generic "magic buttons" that try to replace them. Focus on customization and integration of specialized knowledge.
*   **Supporting Quote**: "If it was true, we wouldn't be hiring, you know, software engineers at record pace as AI has gotten really good at software engineering. It's like, no, AI is making the real specialists more." (Kieran, Transcript 9)

*   **Mainstream View**: Scaling a company means hiring more middle management and expanding administrative overhead.
*   **Contrarian View**: "The interesting thing about Andrew all is what we're doing where we're launching many many many products and where we are Having individual product teams on each of those that are relatively lean is actually easier for me to run as a company Then the companies that have to invest in one or two products and grow them to serve everybody." (Palmer Luckey, Transcript 8)
*   **How to Bet on It**: Adopt a "many small bets" product strategy. Empower small, autonomous product teams (even solo builders) to launch and iterate on many micro-products. This minimizes overhead, fosters agility, and allows for rapid experimentation without the drag of large, centralized structures.
*   **Supporting Quote**: "I'm trying to keep every business unit as close to that size as I can and they're all sort of engineers and product people not And I assume you try to eliminate middle management and there there was a very brief period very early in Andrew's history where we had more Lawyers and lobbyists and engineers."
