- **Moat**:

    - **Prompt Engineering Expertise**: Proprietary methods for effectively extracting verifiable claims from AI output.
    - **User Feedback Loop**: Continuously improve claim extraction and source matching based on user corrections.

## 6. CONTRARIAN TAKES

- **Mainstream View**: AI is making everyone a generalist, democratizing knowledge and allowing anyone to research anything.
- **Contrarian View**: AI (in its current form) actually amplifies the intelligence of *domain experts*, making them more effective at prompting and verifying, while still being unreliable for general research by novices.
- **How to Bet on It**: Build specialized AI tools that require or leverage deep domain knowledge. Target niche professional communities where expertise is highly valued and verification is critical.
- **Supporting Quote**: "All these people were looking at Deep Research and saying, this is fantastic for researching things you don't know anything about. And I was like, no, it's not. Yeah. It's fantastic. If you need a bunch of material about something you know a lot about." (Benedict Evans)

- **Mainstream View**: The internet (and new tech) will destroy all old industries.
- **Contrarian View**: New technologies primarily disrupt specific *parts* of an industry's value chain, while core assets or functions remain resilient.
- **How to Bet on It**: Instead of aiming for total disruption, identify specific, high-friction points within existing industries that can be optimized or replaced by new tech (e.g., travel agents, not airlines; payment rails, not core banking).
- **Supporting Quote**: "The iPhone demolished the existing cellular industry, really having the effect on telcos... The same thing with like online travel booking, completely demolished the travel agent industry didn't really change the airline business." (Benedict Evans)

- **Mainstream View**: Blockchain's killer apps will be consumer-facing social networks or games.
- **Contrarian View**: Blockchain's most impactful and revenue-generating applications are in "boring" areas like international finance, verifiable data custody, and global capital formation.
- **How to Bet on It**: Focus on B2B applications where trust, transparency, and efficient cross-border transactions are critical. Build tools that solve real financial or compliance pain points, not just speculative consumer trends.
- **Supporting Quote**: "Where crypto really shines is the alternative forms of traffic... For example, if you're a power user of money, if I want to receive or send a wire to a startup in Japan, US DC, I can do that in seconds." (Balaji)
