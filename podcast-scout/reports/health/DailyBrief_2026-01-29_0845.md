## 1. HIGH-IMPACT PROTOCOLS

**Protocol Name**: Omega-3 Fatty Acid Supplementation for Brain Health
**Category**: Supplements / Longevity
**The Protocol**: 1-2 grams of DHA (or combined EPA/DHA) daily. Can be achieved through 2-3 good servings of seafood per week or a reasonable supplement.
**Mechanism (The WHY)**: DHA concentrates at neuron synapses and in mitochondria, critical for regulating neurotransmitter release and energy production. It's a precursor for signaling molecules like resolvins and protectins, important for regulating immune response and switching off inflammation, particularly relevant after brain injury. Adequate omega-3 status is also crucial for brain structure and function, and interacts with B vitamin status to support methylation, which is needed for DHA to function correctly in cell membranes.
**Evidence Level**: Moderate (observational studies, some randomized trials in specific populations like football players)
**Expected Benefits**: Reduced accumulation of brain injury markers, decreased risk of dementia and cognitive decline, improved mitochondrial function, and enhanced synaptic function.
**How to Monitor**: Blood tests for omega-3 index, neurofilament light (research setting), cognitive function assessments.
**Cautions**: Consult a physician, especially if on blood thinners.
**Supporting Quote(s)**:
"When you look at long-term omega-3 supplementation, there was a study that was done in football players where they randomized them to different levels of DHA across the season. And they found that those taking one to two grams of DHA a day saw less of an accumulation of a marker of brain injury in the blood called neurofilament light across the season." (Dr. Tommy Wood)
"There absolutely seems to be this interaction between omega-3 status and B vitamin status, particularly the B vitamins that are involved in methylation." (Dr. Tommy Wood)

**Protocol Name**: Creatine Supplementation for Cognitive Function
**Category**: Supplements / Longevity / Mental Performance
**The Protocol**: 10 grams daily, taken in one go, preferably in the morning to avoid sleep disruption.
**Mechanism (The WHY)**: Creatine is cognitively stimulating. While the perfect trial for dementia prevention is lacking, data across depression and sleep deprivation are promising. It is believed to support brain energy metabolism.
**Evidence Level**: Preliminary (interesting data across depression, sleep deprivation; one pediatric TBI trial)
**Expected Benefits**: Enhanced recovery from brain injury, cognitive stimulation, potential benefits in depression and sleep deprivation.
**How to Monitor**: Subjective cognitive performance, sleep quality.
**Cautions**: May cause GI distress in high doses or with low-quality supplements. May be stimulating and affect sleep if taken late in the day.
**Supporting Quote(s)**:

"If I take creatine later in the day, I don't sleep as well. It's very noticeable for me, but that's not the case for everybody." (Dr. Tommy Wood)

## 2. EMERGING PROTOCOLS

**Protocol Name**: High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) for Hippocampal Health
**Category**: Exercise / Longevity
**The Protocol**: 4x4 minute intervals at 85-95% maximum heart rate, with 4 minutes rest in between, 3 times a week for several months.
**Mechanism (The WHY)**: Produces significant lactate, which enters the brain and acts as a histone deacetylase inhibitor, activating BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor) and improving hippocampal structure and function.
**Evidence Level**: Preliminary (one long-duration Australian study)
**Expected Benefits**: Significant improvements in hippocampal structure and function, potentially maintained for several years.
**How to Monitor**: Maximal heart rate zones, subjective exertion (misery-related), cognitive tests.
**Cautions**: This is a very intense protocol. Requires medical clearance and careful progression, especially for individuals not accustomed to high-intensity exercise. Risk of injury.
**What's Missing**: More widespread studies with diverse populations to confirm efficacy and establish optimal protocols for different fitness levels.

**Performance Tips**:

They emphasize the lack of liquidity and flexibility compared to renting.
*   **Home Ownership is a Key Wealth Builder**: David Bach argues that homeowners in America are worth 40 times more than renters. He points to significant home equity growth (e.g., 400% in 20 years, 90% since COVID-19). He emphasizes the leverage of borrowing most of the home's value, tax benefits (deductions, capital gains exclusion), and the forced savings mechanism of mortgage payments. He contends that renters often don't invest the "extra" money they save by not owning, instead spending it on lifestyle creep.
**Practical Takeaway**: For most ordinary people, home ownership is a significant wealth builder due to leverage, tax benefits, and forced savings, even if direct appreciation seems low. The alternative (renting and investing the difference) often fails in practice due to lack of discipline. However, geographic mobility for career opportunities should be considered, though selling a home can be quicker than breaking a lease in some markets, or renting out the owned property is an option.
